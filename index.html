The Core System

Walk · Log · Recap · Track.

Every BuilderPilot tool plugs into one simple daily loop. Learn the loop and the whole system clicks: nothing falls through, and the record builds itself.

Phase 01

Walk

A morning pass of every active lot. One voice note per block — stage, anything outstanding, anything that changed.

Start of Day · Zero extra time
Phase 02

Log

Capture events as they happen — a deficiency, a no-show, a delivery, a direction given. One line each, all day.

Throughout the Day · ~30 sec each
Phase 03

Recap

Drop the day's raw notes into the system, review the formatted output, and send a clean, defensible report.

End of Day · 10–15 min
Phase 04

Track

Outstanding items carry forward to tomorrow until they're confirmed closed and photographed. Nothing slips.

Rolling · Built into the recap

Walk without Log is observing, not documenting. Log without Recap is collecting, not communicating. The whole point of the loop is that nothing falls through.

A Day in the Life

6:45 AM to 4:30 PM.
The Day, Annotated.

It's not a job description — it's how the day actually moves on a low-rise residential site, written by someone who's lived it.

6:45 AM

Arrive On Site

First in — that's non-negotiable. Espresso on before the guys punch in. The first few minutes are the best read you'll get on the day ahead. Your to-do list was built yesterday, not this morning.

8:00 AM

The Morning Walk

A full loop — every unit, every floor. Boots in the kitchen of every house; that one checkpoint forces you through the whole unit. Confirm lock-ups, inspection readiness, and no overnight surprises.

9:30 AM

Coffee Truck & Check-In

Leaders eat last — you wait until every person on site has gone ahead of you. It's a small thing. Everyone notices it. By now you've heard from the inspector on timing.

Mid-Morning

Office Work — Schedules, Orders, Confirmations

Update progress trackers, place material orders, follow up. The double-confirmation rule: emailed a schedule? Call. Called? Email. An unconfirmed delivery is just a wish.

Afternoon

Inspections + QC Walk

Be present and have documentation ready. After the inspector clears, you walk again — a quality-control sweep. Mark up trade work while they're still on site; nobody wants a callback trip.

4:30 PM

End of Day

Confirm what got done, hand out tomorrow's tasks so nobody shows up without direction. Recap the day into the system, lock the office last, and walk the site before you drive off. Then do it again tomorrow.
