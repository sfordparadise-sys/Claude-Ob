ON1Call Locates: Why Every Ontario Site Super Takes This Seriously

Underground locates are mandatory by law in Ontario before any excavation. Here's what you need to know — the colours, the process, your obligations under OHSA, and what happens when someone skips the call.

If you've been on an Ontario construction site for more than a week, you've heard about locates. But familiarity breeds complacency, and complacency on this particular topic puts people in the ground — literally and professionally.

ON1Call is Ontario's one-call notification system. When you submit a locate request, ON1Call notifies all member utilities operating underground in that area. Those utilities send out their locators, who mark the ground with spray paint and flags. You do not touch the ground until you have those marks.

What the Law Actually Says

Under the Ontario Underground Infrastructure Notification System Act (OUINSA), any person who intends to excavate must notify ON1Call at least three working days before breaking ground — unless it's an emergency. This is not a guideline. It is a legal requirement, and violations can result in significant fines under the Act.

OHSA layers on top of this. As a site supervisor, you are a "supervisor" under the Act and carry personal liability for ensuring workers under your direction comply with the law and with safe work practices. If a worker strikes an underground utility and an incident results, the supervisor's failure to secure locates will be examined.

Three working days minimum. Submit the locate request before the weekend if you're breaking ground Monday. "I forgot" is not a defence that has ever held up.

Understanding the Colours

Ontario follows the APWA (American Public Works Association) colour code for underground utility markings. Every site supervisor should know these without having to look them up:

Colour Utility
Red Electric power lines, cables, conduit, and lighting cables
Pink Temporary survey markings
Orange Telecommunications, alarm or signal lines, cables or conduit
Yellow Natural gas, oil, steam, petroleum, or gaseous materials
Green Sewers and drain lines
Blue Potable water
Purple Reclaimed water, irrigation, and slurry lines
White Proposed excavation area

The Locate Process Step by Step

  1. Submit your request. You can call 1-800-400-2255 or submit online at on1call.com. Provide the excavation address, the type of work, the start date, and the excavation area. Be specific about depth — this matters.
  2. Wait the required time. Three working days, minimum. This is not negotiable. If utilities haven't responded within that window, contact ON1Call.
  3. Receive your confirmation. You'll get a locate ticket number. Keep it. This is your proof of compliance.
  4. Verify all utilities have responded. Not just that you got a ticket — that every utility in the area has either marked or cleared the zone.
  5. Respect the marks. Expose by hand within 1 metre of a marked line. Do not assume depth. Do not use power equipment within that tolerance zone.

What "Positive Response" Means

ON1Call's positive response system requires member utilities to respond to every locate request — either by marking in the field or by confirming they have no infrastructure in the area. Before you excavate, every utility on your ticket should show a positive response. If one is outstanding, you wait or you call.

No response is not clearance. An unresponded utility is not proof there's nothing there. It means you don't know. You don't dig when you don't know.

When Marks Aren't Enough

Locates tell you where the utility is horizontally. They tell you nothing precise about depth. The actual depth depends on installation history, soil movement, and whether anyone followed specs when the line went in — which is a significant assumption on old infrastructure.

Within 1 metre of any marked utility, the OHSA requirement is to expose by hand. Hydrovac is the preferred method on production residential sites where there's schedule pressure but the risk of a gas strike is real. Using an excavator within that zone without manual exposure is an OHSA violation, not just a bad call.

Your CYA Documentation

Keep a locate file for every project. It should include:

  • Your ON1Call ticket number and submission confirmation
  • The date submitted and the earliest permitted dig date
  • Positive response confirmations from each utility
  • Photos of the marks in place before excavation begins
  • Notes of any conversations with locators about uncertain areas

If something goes wrong, that file is the difference between an investigation that clears you and one that doesn't.

Call before you dig. Every time. No exceptions.
